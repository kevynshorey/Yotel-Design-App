What is the One Idea?

Every Foster + Partners project begins with a single organizing concept — the "big move" that makes everything else inevitable. What is Mt Brevitor's?

A
Concept A

The Coral Spine

A single landscape corridor runs NE–SW along the trade wind axis. Clusters flank it on both sides, pools nestle into the spine as water features. The entire estate breathes as one bioclimatic system.

B
Concept B

The Three Ridges

Three parallel ridge bands — Premium, Mid, Entry — separated by green valley corridors. Each ridge has a distinct character and view orientation. The valleys are shared landscape and movement corridors.

C
Concept C

The Agricultural Frame

The farm, X Range, and food forest form a protective outer ring. All six clusters face inward toward a sheltered agrarian interior. The estate is enclosed — a walled garden at landscape scale.

Foster + Partners Lens

F+P always ask: "What is the estate doing to the landscape, and what is the landscape doing to the estate?" The best answer eliminates the question — they become inseparable. Which concept most convincingly makes architecture and landscape one thing?
