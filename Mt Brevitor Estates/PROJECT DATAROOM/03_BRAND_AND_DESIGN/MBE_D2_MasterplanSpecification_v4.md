## 1. Executive Summary

Mt Brevitor Estates is a 132.61-acre (gross) satellite-validated masterplan on an elevated plateau in St Peter, Barbados. The development comprises 485 residential units across 7 distinct clusters, a 17-acre working agri-estate, an X Range golf entertainment facility positioned on the southern agricultural strip, a plantation-era reservoir serving as the community hub focal point, a restored plantation building as the Estate Club, and a cleared eastern hilltop delivering 270-degree panoramic Caribbean views from a new premium cluster.

The masterplan is organised around what the site itself demands: the plantation-era reservoir as the centrepiece of the community hub, the sweeping historic estate drive as the alignment basis for the primary boulevard, the cleared hilltop as the natural premium address, and the White Hall Plantation Yard on the southern boundary as the heritage narrative anchor.

Vuemont Country Estate — which borders the north boundary road directly — validates pricing at every tier. Density at 4.0 units per acre (net developable) is well below the Barbados planning ceiling of 6-8 units per acre, providing significant planning headroom.

## 2. Site Overview

### 2.1 Location and Access

Mount Brevitor, St Peter Parish, Barbados — an elevated plateau and hillside at approximately 250-400ft above sea level. The site sits approximately 3km east of Speightstown on the northwest coast.

**Primary Access — North Gate:**
The site is entered from the north via French Village / Mount Brevitor Road, which connects directly to Ronald Mapp Highway (H1). The gatehouse and primary boulevard originate at this northern entry point and curve south-eastward through the open parkland before reaching the community hub at the plantation reservoir. This is the only primary vehicular entry. A secondary service gate will serve the southern agricultural and X Range district, keeping service traffic off residential roads.

This corrects a fundamental v3 error, which placed the gate at the south boundary.

### 2.2 Terrain and Landscape Character

The site comprises three distinct landscape zones:

**North-Central Open Parkland (approx. 40 acres):**
Predominantly open grassland with mature specimen trees. Contains the plantation-era reservoir (approximately 1,200m2) in the north-central zone and an existing plantation-era building in the central area. A historic estate drive sweeps through this open parkland, and its alignment directly informs the routing of the primary estate boulevard. This zone will form the heart of the community hub.

**Eastern Hilltop (approx. 25 acres):**
Satellite imagery confirms this elevation was previously described as mature tropical woodland — this was incorrect. The eastern hilltop is covered in brush and weed trees requiring clearance, not protected canopy. Clearance will reveal panoramic 270-degree Caribbean views across the west and north coasts. This site characteristic, once recognised, transforms the eastern elevation from a zone of minimal development potential into the highest-value address on the site. Cluster G (The Hilltop) occupies this zone.

**Southern Agricultural Strip (approx. 30+ acres):**
Lower-lying agricultural land bordering White Hall Plantation Yard on the southern H1 frontage. This strip accommodates the X Range golf entertainment district and the 17-acre agri-estate. Its southern and eastern position relative to residential clusters, combined with north-easterly trade wind alignment, means noise and activity are carried away from homes. The White Hall Plantation Yard heritage corridor on the southern boundary provides an authentic narrative anchor for the district.

**Gully System (22.52 acres, non-buildable):**
The eastern gully is a non-buildable constraint delineating the eastern and south-eastern site boundary. It functions as a natural buffer, habitat corridor, and scenic edge. Cluster boundaries are set back from the gully lip. The gully informs a network of pedestrian trails along its northern edge.

## 3. Masterplan Concept

### 3.1 The Organising Idea: The Site Tells You What It Wants to Be

The v4 masterplan is derived from what the site itself presents. Each major planning decision follows directly from a confirmed site feature.

**The reservoir is the hub.** The plantation-era reservoir in the north-central parkland is approximately 1,200m2 of still water within open grassland — a natural gathering point. The community hub is positioned facing it. The reservoir becomes the estate's visual and social heart.

**The estate drive is the boulevard.** A sweeping historic estate drive already traces the natural movement corridor through open parkland from the northern gate toward the central open area. The primary estate boulevard follows this alignment rather than imposing a new line across the landscape.

**The hilltop is the premium address.** The cleared eastern elevation delivers unobstructed 270-degree views across the Caribbean that no other cluster can replicate. Cluster G occupies this position as the estate's flagship address.

**White Hall is the heritage anchor.** The southern boundary's White Hall Plantation Yard provides authentic narrative depth to the X Range and agri-estate district, grounding the development in Barbadian plantation history.

### 4.3 Cluster Descriptions

**Cluster A — Springhouse Green (80 units, North Entry Zone)**
The entry cluster positioned immediately inside the north gate. Product: 1-bed condominiums at BBD 370,000. The most accessible price tier, designed to maximise early pre-sales velocity and establish site activity rapidly. Named for the natural spring character of the north parkland. First cluster to complete in Phase 1. Faces the approach boulevard and benefits from proximity to both the north gate and the community hub.

**Cluster B — The Terrace (120 units, West-Central Parkland)**
The largest cluster by unit count, occupying the west-central parkland. Product: 2-bed townhouses at BBD 560,000. Positioned to capture prevailing north-east trade wind cooling and views west toward the coast. Cluster B anchors Phase 2 delivery and provides the estate's broadest market reach. The Terrace amenity pavilion offers pool bar, fitness lawn, and dog park.

**Cluster C — The Pavilion (110 units, Hub-Adjacent, Faces Reservoir)**
The hub-facing cluster directly overlooking the plantation-era reservoir. Product: 3-bed townhouses. The most social cluster, positioned to take advantage of the reservoir's visual amenity and proximity to the central hub facilities. Phase 2 delivery. The Pavilion amenity building faces the water and includes jacuzzi and full event pavilion.

**Cluster D — Terrace Club (55 units, Eastern Hilltop Edge)**
Upper-elevation cluster at the outer edge of the eastern hilltop. Product: 4-bed homes at BBD 880,000. Positioned between the main plateau and the cleared hilltop. Provides the direct buggy track connection (1.2km) to the X Range District below. Phase 3 delivery. Terrace Club amenity includes 30m pool, sundeck, cabanas, and golf buggy staging.

**Cluster E — The Estate (40 units, Deep Eastern Hilltop Premium)**
The deepest and most elevated cluster, occupying the inner section of the eastern hilltop. Product: 5-bed estate homes at BBD 1,750,000. Phase 4 delivery, premium by scarcity. The Estate infinity pool faces west, capturing evening light across the Caribbean. Concierge service, wine cellar, private dining, and farm-box subscription included in amenity programme.

**Cluster F — Social Club (50 units, South-West, White Hall Heritage District)**
The south-western cluster adjacent to the White Hall Plantation Yard and X Range district. Product: mixed residential. Positioned to capitalise on heritage narrative and proximity to social amenities. The Social Club pavilion includes a rooftop terrace designed to the YOTEL convertible brief (flex use). Phase 2 delivery.

**Cluster G — The Hilltop (50 units, Cleared Eastern Summit) — NEW IN v4**
The seventh cluster occupying the cleared eastern hilltop between Clusters D and E. Satellite imagery confirmed that the previously described mature tropical woodland is in fact brush and weed trees — clearable without heritage or protected-species restriction (subject to ecological survey: see Open Items). Clearance will expose unobstructed 270-degree panoramic views across the Caribbean, the north coast, and inland St Peter.

Product mix:
- 10 x 3-bed TH at BBD 623,700 = BBD 6,237,000
- 25 x 4-bed home at BBD 880,000 = BBD 22,000,000
- 15 x 5-bed estate at BBD 1,750,000 = BBD 26,250,000
- **Cluster G GDV total: BBD 54,487,000**

Capex: BBD ~12,000,000 (clearance, hillside infrastructure, road, amenity building). Phase 4 delivery.

The Hilltop cluster amenity includes a 25m infinity pool with jacuzzi and panoramic viewing terrace — the highest-elevation pool on the estate.

**Boulevard Routing:**
The primary boulevard enters from the north gate, curves south-east through open parkland following the historic estate drive alignment, passes the restored plantation building (Estate Club), and terminates at the community hub plaza facing the plantation reservoir. The curve is not a designer imposition — it is the alignment the plantation established over generations.

**Buggy Track — Cluster D to X Range:**
A 1.2km dedicated golf buggy and pedestrian track connects Cluster D (Terrace Club) to the X Range District on the southern agricultural strip. Segregated from road traffic. Residents access X Range informally without a car journey.

**Hilltop Connections:**
A dedicated 3m coral stone trail loop connects Clusters D, G, and E on the eastern hilltop, providing pedestrian and buggy circulation at elevation. Clusters G and E are accessed via collector spur roads; the trail loop provides secondary connectivity and a scenic perimeter route.

## 7. X Range District — XRU Format

### 7.1 Position and Site Rationale

The X Range golf entertainment district is located on the **southern agricultural strip**, approximately 6 acres, adjacent to the White Hall Plantation Yard heritage corridor on the H1 frontage. This is a confirmed location based on satellite site analysis.

This position satisfies all X Range operational and brand requirements simultaneously:

- **South-facing bays:** The elongated bay building sits at the northern edge of the 6-acre strip; bays face **south**, firing into the agricultural outfield. NE trade winds carry range noise south-westward and away from all residential clusters (all of which sit north and west of the X Range).
- **Mega Media Outfield:** ~18,700 m² grass outfield extends south from the bay building — sufficient for the full Laservision illuminated target circuit. No residential within the 170m outfield depth.
- **Heritage context:** White Hall Plantation Yard on the southern boundary provides an authentic backdrop and F&B narrative anchor. The range district is grounded in Barbadian plantation history.
- **Segregated access:** H1 service gate (south) serves both X Range and agri-estate — no golf or agricultural traffic on residential roads.
- **Resident buggy connection from Cluster D:** 1.2km dedicated buggy track, 5-minute ride.

## 8. Sustainability and Resilience

### 8.1 Agrivoltaic Solar

A 2-3 MW agrivoltaic solar installation is planned for the south-facing lower slope. Dual-use with food crops directly beneath panel arrays.

- Estimated power generation: 2-3 MW
- Supplies common areas and EV/buggy charging infrastructure
- Feed-in tariff income to reduce operating cost
- Integrated with agri-estate crop production below panels

### 8.2 Agri-Estate (17 Acres)

- Aquaponics: tilapia and vegetables
- Dairy and artisan cheese production
- Farm shop and farm-to-table supply to cluster amenity kitchens and Cluster E residents
- Stabilised commercial revenue: BBD 2.5M per annum

### 8.3 Water Management

- Rainwater cisterns: 30,000 gallons total capacity across all clusters
- Plantation reservoir: preserved as aesthetic feature and emergency water storage
- Grey-water recycling for landscaping irrigation across all cluster amenity areas

### 8.4 Hurricane Resilience

- All structures designed to Barbados Building Code hurricane standards
- Underground utilities throughout — no overhead lines
- Reinforced roof ties on all townhouses and estate homes
- Emergency shelter capacity within central hub building: 10-15% of total resident population
- Backup generator provision at hub and Estate Club
